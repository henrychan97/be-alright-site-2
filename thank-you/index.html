⚠️ Please read before your call

We only take a few calls each week.

Be Alright isn't a call center. Every consultation is run one-on-one by a reading specialist — one family at a time. That's what makes these calls so helpful, but it also means there are very few spots.

Because the calendar is small and in high demand, a missed call usually can't be rebooked for weeks — and your time is given to another family who's waiting and worried about their child. Please treat this like an appointment you can't miss.

  • 📅Add it to your calendar right now, while you're thinking about it.
  • ⏰Set a reminder for 10 minutes before so you're not rushing.
  • 📞Need to move it? Tell us at least 24 hours ahead so we can give your spot to another family.
Before we talk

How to get the most from your call

It's a relaxed, no-pressure chat — usually about 20–30 minutes. A little prep helps us help your child faster.

1

Find a quiet spot

Somewhere you can talk freely for 20–30 minutes without too many interruptions.

2

Think about the struggle

Jot down what worries you most — the tears, the avoiding, the words that aren't sticking.

3

Bring any reports

Have any school notes, report cards, or an IEP nearby if you have them. Totally fine if you don't.

4

Show up ready

Come with your questions. You'll leave knowing exactly what's going on — even if you never enroll.

Quick questions before your call

How long is the consultation?
About 20 to 30 minutes. Enough time to really understand your child — not so long that it eats your whole day.
Who will I be talking to?
One of our reading specialists — the same caring people who work with kids every day, not a sales floor.
What actually happens on the call?
We listen. You tell us what's been hard, we ask a few questions, and we give you an honest read on what's going on and what would help — even if that's something you do on your own. No pressure, ever.
What should I have ready?
Just a sense of what worries you most about your child's reading, and any school reports or an IEP if you happen to have them. If you don't, no problem at all.
Where does the call happen?
By phone or video, wherever you're comfortable. Check your email and texts — your link and time are there. Add it to your calendar so it's easy to find.
What if I really need to reschedule?
Life happens — just let us know at least 24 hours ahead. Because spots are so limited, a missed call often can't be rebooked for a while, so a heads-up lets us offer your time to another waiting family.
